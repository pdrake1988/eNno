Find an Online Broker

GameStop is only available on the NYSE, which means you need an online broker with access to this exchange. A characteristic of brokers is what exchanges they have access to; not all Brokers allow buying shares in GameStop because some don’t even offer trades in America’s largest market – The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE). Open an Account

You can open an account with any online broker. However, it’s more like opening a new Gmail than anything else. Some people might need to do background checks or have the fund’s transfer take a while (or both), so keep in mind that this may affect how quickly you get your shares.

Deposit Some Amount in Your Account

To buy GameStop’s stocks, you will need to send cash through your broker. This process is usually quick and easy – even easier than opening up an account. Deposit money into your investment account using a bank transfer, credit/debit card, or electronic wallet. Some brokers allow you to do this even from different wallets like PayPal.

Buy The Share

You’re ready to invest in GameStop, so you log into your online brokerage and search for shares. Once the buy button appears, it’s just a matter of placing an order. When placing orders, you can choose from different order types. The market order buys at the actual price of a share while limit ones let users specify exact prices they want for their purchases.

Review Your Position Regularly

GameStop is a great company to invest in, but you needn’t wait until after their next annual meeting. Monitor investments and follow strategy by following how much they cost at different times throughout history. The most important thing to consider when using different position management tools is that they should be used in conjunction. For example, you can set your target price at which it would like to sell shares with a profit or use stop-losses to avoid losing any more money on investment than necessary.
